DAEDALUS — Trust Architecture

Retrieval efficiency and knowledge trustworthiness are orthogonal problems. Every domain that uses AI to process unstructured data into decisions faces the same trust gap. DAEDALUS solves it structurally — with pluggable constraint sets, a universal trust model, and domain-independent corrective feedback. One architecture, three domains.

The Problem

Engineers using AI assistants for battery cell design face three compounding failures:

  1. Amnesia — AI starts every session from zero. Context, constraints, and prior decisions must be re-explained each time.
  2. Blind Trust — AI-generated specifications carry no provenance. A verified material property and a hallucinated value appear with identical confidence.
  3. Silent Failure — LLMs generate designs with parameters outside manufacturable range, impossible ratios, or energy density claims violating thermodynamic limits — presented as valid.
BLUE Verified engineering fact Material spec from datasheet, validated test result, confirmed constraint
GREEN AI-suggested, pending validation "Based on prior sessions, optimal cathode thickness is ~120µm"
RED Conflict or constraint violation Design proposes N/P ratio of 0.95 — below minimum safe threshold of 1.05

The Problem

Customer service and after-sales operations teams process massive volumes of unstructured data — dealer reports, warranty claims, complaints, diagnostic logs, voice transcripts — using NLP/GenAI pipelines. Three trust failures emerge:

  1. Signal Confusion — AI extracts "trends" from unstructured data, but there's no distinction between a verified quality issue and an AI-inferred correlation from noisy reports.
  2. Hallucination Cascade — LLM infers pattern A, stores as insight, retrieves as context for next analysis, compounds into confident but wrong recommendation.
  3. Unvalidated Recommendations — GenAI suggests service procedure changes based on trend analysis. No automated check against engineering constraints, warranty policy, or parts compatibility.
BLUE Confirmed by root-cause analysis Confirmed battery defect in production batch #4721, official service procedure SP-2024-089
GREEN AI-inferred trend, pending validation "Dealer reports suggest 23% increase in charging complaints in Nordic markets — possible correlation with firmware v3.2"

The Problem

Benchmarking teams analyze competitors to predict technology roadmaps, production capacity, and strategic direction. Intelligence is gathered from job postings, patent filings, earnings calls, supplier announcements, trade press. Three trust failures:

  1. Inference as Fact — An analyst or AI infers a strategic pivot from a cluster of job postings. Over time, this inference gets cited as established fact in reports, influencing strategy without anyone flagging the original evidence quality.
  2. Source Conflicts Buried — One patent suggests Direction A, a supplier announcement implies Direction B. Without trust grading, the most recent or most confident source wins, not the most reliable.
  3. Temporal Decay Ignored — A competitive insight from 2024 is treated as current in 2026. Without bi-temporal reasoning, outdated intelligence persists.
BLUE Confirmed by multiple independent sources Annual report confirms 100 GWh capacity expansion, confirmed patent grant
GREEN AI-inferred from pattern matching "Job postings suggest competitor building sodium-ion team in Munich — possible European market entry"
RED Sources conflict or data is stale AI infers capacity reduction, but latest earnings call confirms expansion
